Malware Families as Dynamical Systems: Why Drift Detection Outperforms Feature-Space Forecasting

Noor Muhannad · Independent Researcher

This work models malware families as temporal trajectories in EMBER feature space. Across 43 temporally supported families, moving-average forecasting reached 0.820 mean cosine similarity, while a streaming drift-jump detector achieved 0.73 F1. The central conclusion is that anomaly detection on drift is more actionable than short-horizon point forecasting.
